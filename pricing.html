How Pricing Works

A straightforward structure matched to your team.

Ledgewave is typically proposed as an annual software subscription plus a separate implementation plan. That keeps pricing tied to the files, workflow complexity, reporting needs, and onboarding work required to launch well.

Software subscription

  • Presented as an annual subscription
  • Aligned to the workflow and reporting needs for the initial launch
  • Designed for teams with meaningful receivables volume or follow-up complexity
  • Expanded as additional workflow needs are prioritized

Implementation and onboarding

  • Planned separately from the software subscription
  • Includes workflow review, data intake planning, and environment-specific setup
  • Supports available receivables data, planned billing inputs, and reporting requirements
  • Guided by a managed onboarding process from review through launch
Pricing Drivers

What shapes the proposal.

The same practical factors usually determine the right plan, timeline, and pricing for a deployment.

Receivables and invoice volume

The size of the working book and the operational load on the team influence software coverage and onboarding effort.

Data intake needs

Receivables files, planned billing inputs, reporting needs, and customer-specific ingest paths all affect the rollout plan.

Workflow complexity

Draft follow-up stages, approval steps, review cadence, and forecast depth help define the launch configuration.

User footprint

The mix of operators, managers, reviewers, and finance leaders helps shape access, reporting, and support needs.

Implementation depth

A file-based launch is different from a broader deployment that also includes customer-specific API ingest or deeper reporting.

Support expectations

Onboarding cadence, review sessions, and ongoing support expectations are reflected in the final plan.

Pricing FAQ

Questions teams usually ask before requesting pricing.

Do you offer public pricing tiers?

Ledgewave pricing is tailored to each deployment because workflow coverage, data intake, onboarding needs, and implementation requirements can vary by team.

What term length should we expect?

Most first deployments begin as annual software subscriptions, with final terms confirmed in the proposal.

Is implementation included in the software fee?

Implementation and onboarding are planned separately from the software subscription so the launch plan matches the data, workflow, and rollout work required.

Can teams start with a focused launch?

Yes. Many teams begin with a focused launch that stabilizes the core receivables workflow before broader enhancements are added.

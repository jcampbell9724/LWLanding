What Buyers Usually Need

Security review is usually less about buzzwords and more about operating discipline.

Most diligence conversations focus on the same practical questions: what data enters the platform, who can access it, how activity stays auditable, and how the rollout fits an enterprise environment.

Security Principles

The trust model starts with reducing operating sprawl around receivables work.

Finance teams usually inherit risk when customer context, follow-up history, and cash expectations are rebuilt manually across multiple tools. Ledgewave is positioned around making that workflow tighter, clearer, and more reviewable.

Keep sensitive workflow context in one operating layer

Reduce the need to duplicate receivables history, promises, disputes, and forecast notes across disconnected trackers.

Apply access with business context in mind

Security decisions should follow role, operational need, and review discipline rather than informal spreadsheet access.

Preserve accountability inside the workflow

Collections activity is more trustworthy when actions, updates, and timing changes stay attributable to named users and known process steps.

Review integrations deliberately

ERP exports, CRM context, payment signals, and downstream reporting should be introduced through defined paths instead of ad hoc data movement.

Match controls to the real deployment

Security posture should be assessed against the actual environment, rollout scope, and data categories involved in the implementation.

Support diligence without marketing shortcuts

Buyers should be able to review how the product is operated without being asked to rely on vague trust language or unverified compliance claims.

Compliance Posture

Use this page as an overview, not as shorthand for a certification claim.

Trust pages are useful when they reduce ambiguity. They become misleading when they imply formal certifications, regulatory coverage, or contractual guarantees that have not been confirmed directly.

What this page is meant to do

  • Summarize the security themes buyers should expect to review
  • Show that diligence is part of the product conversation, not an afterthought
  • Point evaluators to privacy documentation and rollout review paths
  • Frame the areas where finance, IT, procurement, and legal teams usually align

What still needs direct confirmation

  • Any certification status such as SOC 2, ISO 27001, or similar frameworks
  • Any regulatory fit such as HIPAA, PCI DSS, or industry-specific obligations
  • Customer-specific retention, deletion, residency, or subprocessor requirements
  • Any binding commitments related to uptime, response times, or security controls

Privacy alignment

The public website privacy practices are described on the Privacy Policy page. Product-specific handling, retention, and contractual requirements should be reviewed in the context of the planned deployment.

Buyer Workflow

A cleaner diligence sequence usually speeds up the rollout.

The fastest evaluations are the ones that clarify scope first, then review controls against that actual scope instead of forcing both teams into generic questionnaires with no deployment context.

1

Define the data scope

Agree on what receivables, billing, customer, and workflow information would be in scope for the evaluation.

2

Review access and workflow ownership

Clarify who will administer the system, who will operate inside it, and which teams need visibility into activity history.

3

Confirm privacy and vendor requirements

Bring procurement, legal, or compliance reviewers in once the intended use case and data flow are concrete.

4

Set rollout guardrails

Document the agreed operating model before implementation expands into deeper integrations or broader user access.

Security FAQ

Security review questions buyers usually ask early.

These answers make the trust and diligence conversation more concrete before security review becomes a blocker.

What data does Ledgewave handle?

Ledgewave is built for receivables workflows that include customer balances, billing schedules, dispute notes, follow-up history, and cash forecast context.

How does Ledgewave support auditability?

The product is designed to keep notes, workflow activity, and follow-up history attached to the receivable so teams can review what changed, what was sent, and why decisions were made.

When should security review happen?

Security review works best during evaluation, while the team is still defining data inputs, access scope, operating ownership, and rollout guardrails.

Does this page imply a certification claim?

No. The security page is an overview of trust, controls, and diligence themes. It should not be treated as a shorthand certification claim.
